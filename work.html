"30 Sunflowers"

David Hockney has painted several still life paintings of flowers that are dedicated to the passing of his closest friends and including those who are unwell. As seen in the painting, the wilting sunflowers represent his friends who are ill. Though it has a sad meaning, Hockney uses vibrant colours to add a positive feeling in order to mask the sadness he was going through. This painting was displayed with his other flower paintings at an exhibition called “Flowers, Faces and Spaces” at Annely Juda Fine Art in London in 1997.




"I draw flowers every day and send them to my friends so they get fresh blooms every morning."
~ David Hockney


"A bigger splash"

David Hockney - A Bigger Splash 1967" by oddsock is licensed under CC BY 2.0

In 1964, David Hockney moved to California and he was drawn to the hot climate and the bright and relaxed environment. He became inspired to paint one of his most well-known paintings, “A bigger splash” along with his other swimming pool paintings between 1964 and 1971 which he explored painting the changing surface of water. He uses a suitable medium and bright colours to depict California’s hot climate. The splash he painted was based on a photograph and he was amazed how the splash that lasts for only a few seconds can be captured on a photograph. During this period, Hockney was obsessed with painting pools.


"Everyone knows a splash can’t be frozen in time, so when you see it like that in a painting it’s even more striking than in a photograph."
~ David Hockney



"Pool with Two Figures"

"David Hockney" by dwhartwig is licensed under CC BY 2.0

Did you know that this was the most expensive painting to ever be sold at an auction? This painting was sold for $90.3 million in 2018 to an unknown buyer. It is Hockney’s most renowned painting and it was painted after his emotional breakup in 1971 with Peter Schlesinger, who is the man standing in the painting. The painting tells an emotional story of his breakup with Peter and it shows the tension between the two figures where Peter is closing his eyes and looking downwards while Hockney, the man in the pool is unable to see or hear anything which shows the disconnection between the two.


"Everyone knows a splash can’t be frozen in time, so when you see it like that in a painting it’s even more striking than in a photograph."

"American Collectors: Fred and Marcia Weisman"

"David Hockney, American Collectors (Fred and Marcia Weisman), 1968" by Sharon Mollerus is licensed under CC BY 2.0

This piece was completed in 1968 of a couple who are avid art collectors at their Los Angeles home. Interestingly, the painting has a subtle humour because of how they seem to mirror the statues at their home and how far apart they are standing from one another whilst looking serious. In this collection, he explored many styles, mainly lines and creating balance and symmetry with the placement of the statues.


"The portrait wasn’t just in the faces, it was in the whole setting."
~ David Hockney



"Mr and Mrs Clark and Percy"

"David Hockney - Mr and Mrs Clark and Percy" by irinaraquel is licensed under CC BY 2.0

The painting features two of Hockney’s friends, a fashion designer Ossie Clark and his wife who is a textile designer, Celia Birtwell in their flat shortly after their wedding. The painting foreshadows their failed marriage where Celia can be seen standing while Clark was sitting which was unconventional of a wedding portrait. Some symbolism in his paintings are the lilies which represent feminine purity while the cat on Clark’s lap represents someone who does what they please. The open window with the sunlight separating the two foreshadows their divorce afterwards.




"The photograph isn’t good enough. It’s not real enough."
~ David Hockney

Despite his age, David Hockney is keeping up with technology and is very skilled in painting on his Ipad. He has always been interested in technology and he first started experimenting digitally in 1985 with an early computer programme before moving on to paint on Iphone and the Ipad. He mainly drew his surroundings, landscapes and nature as seen in the photos above. He then came up with a book titled, “My Window” which is a collection of his paintings done on the Iphone and Ipad.
