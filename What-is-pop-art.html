Other famous pop art artists

Andy Warhol

He is one of the most influential and well-known artists in the pop art movement and culture in the 1960s.He is famous for using everyday items and turning them into art. Some of his most famous artworks are the Campbell soup cans and Marilyn Diptych. His painting techniques are silkscreen and lithography.

Keith Haring

He is an American painter and he is also influential in the pop art movement along with Andy Warhol. He is famous for painting everyday objects and cartoon comic strips. His paintings are very bold and vibrant and he uses Ben Day dots in his comic paintings.

Roy Lichtenstein

He is an American artist who is famous for his pop art and drawing in subways. He was heavily inspired by graffiti artists. His drawings mostly consist of human illustrations, barking dogs and spaceships. Most of his drawings convey a message and raise awareness of important topics such as AIDS and war.

Brief History of Pop Art

Pop art is an art movement that originated from Britain in the 1950s and eventually made its way to the United States in the 1960s. The word ‘Pop Art’ was derived from the word ‘Popular Culture’. Artists such as Eduardo Palouzzi and Richard Hamilton were the first ones who founded Pop Art. This art movement became prevalent in the 20th century because it combines fine art and imagery which has a modern feel to the artworks. The art style stood out as artists take inspiration from the media such as advertisements, icons, comics and everyday items for the mass audience. Some of the most famous Pop Art artists today are Andy Warhol, Keith Haring including David Hockney which many of their artworks have influenced the Pop Art culture today.
